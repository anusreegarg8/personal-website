About Me

I'm a senior policy strategist and grantmaker who has spent a decade building governance infrastructure for problems the world wasn't yet ready to prioritize. At the U.S. State Department, I helped build the Nature Crimes function, moved a combined $42M+ across illicit finance and environmental crime portfolios, co-authored the first UN resolution establishing an expert group on environmental crimes, and sustained 91% program retention through a 70%+ bureau budget cuts during changing administrations. 

Before State, I served as a Policy Analyst at RAND Corporation co-authoring peer-reviewed reports and spent three years as a Research Officer at the IMF's Financial Integrity Group, serving as desk officer for 30+ countries. I hold a JD with an international law focus from American University, with clerkships at DOJ, the UN Counter-Terrorism Executive Directorate, and DHS.

I'm now applying federal grantmaking discipline, multilateral coalition-building, and a track record of founding new institutional functions from blank pages to AI governance and catastrophic risk philanthropy.
